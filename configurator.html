Bagholder Exit Liquidity Pattern
Pre-market gap fade · Short-side · Alert-only
Stocks that have been in prolonged decline (6–12 months, 50%+ drawdown) create trapped holders. When these stocks suddenly gap up 30%+ in pre-market on no real fundamental catalyst (just social media hype or vague news), the trapped holders sell aggressively at the open, overwhelming speculative buyers. The stock fades back down within 60 minutes.

Entry: Short at or near 9:30 AM ET open  ·  Time stop: Close by 10:30 AM ET  ·  Hard skip: M&A / FDA approval / real earnings beat → DO NOT TRADE regardless of score.

Opening Range Fade (ORF)
Opening range breakdown · Short-side · Alert-only
Stocks that spike above their 9:30–9:45 AM ET opening range high, then break back below it — a high-probability short setup. Trapped buyers from the initial spike are forced to sell when the range breaks, accelerating the move down.

Entry: Short when price closes back below OR high  ·  Stop: 3% above OR high  ·  Target 1: OR low  ·  Target 2: Prior day close  ·  Time stop: 11:00 AM ET.

Earnings Fade Scanner
⚙️ Agent not yet built — configure in advance
Stocks that beat earnings expectations but gap up on weak guidance, one-time items, or already-priced-in results attract sellers immediately. The pattern: stock gaps up 5–20% pre-market, probes the highs in the first 30 minutes, then fails and fades — often back to the prior close or below by 11 AM ET.

Unlike Bagholder (which needs prolonged decline), Earnings Fade works on any stock as long as the "sell the news" dynamic is present. The earnings calendar is pulled fresh each day — no watchlist needed.

Entry: Short at 10:00 AM ET fade confirmation  ·  Stop: 5% above pre-market high  ·  Hard skip: Clean beat (EPS + revenue + raised guidance) → DO NOT TRADE.
≥35/60 → A+ Setup 20–34 → Monitor <20 → Skip
